## Design Context

### Users
Small-to-medium businesses actively shopping for a payment processor. Also serves enterprise clients through a sales-led motion. Operates across verticals (keeping positioning broad for now). Users land here comparing options, evaluating trust signals, and looking for a reason to switch from their current processor. The cashback angle is the key differentiator.

### Brand Personality
**Trustworthy, approachable, premium.** PekoPay should feel like a financial product you'd confidently hand your business to — not cold or corporate, but warm and credible. Think: a well-dressed advisor who speaks plainly.

### Aesthetic Direction
Heavy reference from **Wealthsimple** — warm, editorial, confident typography, lots of breathing room. Secondary references: **Robinhood** (clean, approachable, modern), **Fundrise** (grounded, investment-forward). Stripe is admired internally but was not a direct reference for this build. Light theme preferred. The current cream + gold palette with Fraunces serif is intentional and should be preserved as a foundation. Vancouver, BC-based — subtle Pacific Northwest groundedness.

### Anti-references
Should NOT look like a generic SaaS dashboard marketing page. Should NOT look like a crypto/Web3 product. Should NOT feel cold, clinical, or overly technical.

### Design Principles
1. **Warmth over sterility** — Financial doesn't have to mean cold. Use warm tones, generous spacing, and human language.
2. **Confidence through restraint** — Premium feeling comes from what you leave out, not what you pile on. Let typography and whitespace do the work.
3. **Earn trust visually** — Every element should reinforce credibility. Real numbers, clear structure, no decorative noise.
4. **Approachable complexity** — Payment processing is complex; the site should make it feel simple without being simplistic.
5. **Editorial over corporate** — Closer to a well-designed financial publication than a software product page.
